Scoring

The process of making a crease in paper so it will fold easier. A paper score is a ridge that is indented into the paper where the fold line will occur.

Perforating

This process involves puncturing the material to allow for easy separation of two sections of the material, such as allowing paper to be torn easily along the line.

Custom Die Cutting

Created by using a sharp steel blade formed into a specific shape, then used to cut through the paper. Think of a steel cookie cutter for paper instead of dough.

Collating

Collate printing is the action of taking multiple different printed papers and putting them together into a complete set.

Padding

A binding of multiple sheets by using a flexible adhesive along one edge of a stack of sheets, such as notepads, a memo pad, order pads, etc...

Perfect Binding

The kind of binding you see on paperback books. This type of binding is great for both books and presentations alike.

Saddle Stitching

Secures loose leaf papers down the middle with staples. Saddle stiching is a cost effective way to bind booklets and smaller, less formal materials.
